Quality Gates (Must Pass Before Submission)

Gate 1: Scope Compliance

  • Every DC project has AI as INTEGRAL to research (not instrumental)
  • All DCs receive dedicated AI-in-science training
  • Research results depend on AI capabilities
  • Publications target domain-science venues (not just CS/AI)
  • No Chinese entities in consortium

Gate 2: Template Compliance

  • Part B1 ≤ 34 pages total
  • Sections 1-3 ≤ 30 pages (starting page 5)
  • Table of Contents ≤ 1 page
  • Participating Organisations ≤ 2 pages
  • All 6 mandatory tables present (Table 1, 3.1a-e)
  • All required sub-headings present

Gate 3: Consortium Compliance

  • ≥ 3 independent legal entities from ≥ 3 countries
  • ≥ 1 entity from EU Member State
  • ≤ 540 person-months total
  • ≤ 15 doctoral candidates
  • All partners listed in Part A
  • Associated partners described in Part B1 and B2

Gate 4: Budget Compliance

  • Budget within EUR 1-5M range
  • Person-months match DC table
  • Unit costs use 2026 rates
  • Country correction coefficients applied correctly

Gate 5: Competitive Score Target

  • Excellence section targets 4.8+/5.0
  • Impact section targets 4.8+/5.0
  • Implementation section targets 4.8+/5.0
  • Overall target: 96.3+/100

RAISE-Specific Compliance Checklist

AI Integral to Research (CRITICAL)

For each of the 15 DC projects:

  • AI methods are CORE to research hypothesis (not data processing only)
  • DC project description explains WHY AI is needed
  • Research outcome depends on AI capabilities
  • Expected publications target DOMAIN science venues (50%+)
  • Domain supervisor is primary, not secondary
  • Training includes domain-specific + AI methodology courses

RAISE Training Curriculum

  • Mandatory AI in science module (all 15 DCs)
  • Domain-specific AI applications course (tailored to 3-4 science areas)
  • Responsible AI & ethics module
  • Hands-on AI tool/framework training (2+ weeks)
  • Advanced courses in specialization area
  • Secondment opportunities with AI industry partners
  • Career development and entrepreneurship module
  • Public engagement and science communication
  • Table 1 lists all training events with dates and learning outcomes

RAISE Network Structure

  • Network Coordinator clearly identified
  • Scientific Advisory Board role described
  • Training Committee or Working Group mentioned
  • DC governance and decision-making process defined
  • Industry/innovation partners engaged (not just academic)
  • Section 5 (Management) addresses RAISE-specific governance

RAISE Deliverables

  • D1: Inaugural AI-in-science training programme
  • D2: DC research publications (domain science venues)
  • D3: Open science/data outputs
  • D4: Network sustainability plan (post-project)
  • D5: Joint industry secondment programmes
  • D6: Public engagement materials
  • Deliverables table includes dissemination level & type

RAISE Ethics (Section 4)

  • AI ethics as cross-cutting theme
  • Domain-specific ethical implications of AI addressed
  • GDPR compliance for any data projects
  • Responsible AI principles integrated in training
  • Plan for DCs to address ethical aspects in research plans

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Scope & Framing Pitfalls

  • ❌ AI treated as data processing tool only (not core research)
  • ❌ All DCs identical with superficial AI modifications
  • ❌ Insufficient emphasis on domain science outcomes
  • ❌ Missing training curriculum or superficial training plan
  • ❌ DC projects don't match stated research objectives
  • ❌ Too few industrial/non-academic partners

Content Pitfalls

  • ❌ Section 1.1 exceeds 10 pages (forces cutting other sections)
  • ❌ Tables incomplete or mismatched (esp. Table 3.1d DC table)
  • ❌ Supervision structure unclear or insufficient diversity
  • ❌ No secondment strategy or weak secondment plans
  • ❌ Governance section doesn't address RAISE-specific coordination
  • ❌ Ethics section mentions AI only in passing

Formal/Compliance Pitfalls

  • ❌ Part B1 exceeds 34 pages (automatic desk rejection)
  • ❌ Missing mandatory table or section
  • ❌ Consortium <3 partners or <3 countries
  • ❌ Budget misaligned with person-months
  • ❌ Chinese entities in consortium (non-associated only)
  • ❌ No or weak Associate Partner engagement
  • ❌ ToC page numbering not aligned with actual document

Competitive Quality Pitfalls

  • ❌ Generic training programme (copy-paste from 2025 proposal)
  • ❌ Weak dissemination strategy (no clear target audiences)
  • ❌ Insufficient detail on DC projects (< 150 words each average)
  • ❌ No innovation or novel training elements
  • ❌ Weak connections to European skills agendas or policy

Submission Process Step-by-Step

Step 1: Portal Registration (by May 30)

  1. Log into Funding & Tenders Portal (F&T)
  2. Create new proposal (HORIZON-RAISE-2026-01-03)
  3. Fill in basic info: Acronym, title, duration (4 years)
  4. Select Call: RAISE Doctoral Networks
  5. Add all consortium members as users
  6. Tick "RAISE Doctoral Networks" interest box
  7. Set coordinator as submitter

Step 2: Part A - Administrative (by Nov 20)

  1. Online form completion:
    • Project abstract (250 words)
    • Project title
    • Call identifier (HORIZON-RAISE-2026-01-03)
    • Duration: 48 months
    • Budget: EUR 1-5M
    • Person-months: ≤540 total
    • Doctoral candidates: 15
  • Add all partners and associated partners
  • Designate coordinator as contact point
  • Upload supporting documents (CVs, org descriptions)

    • Step 3: Part B1 - Technical Proposal (by Nov 20)

    1. Download Part B1 Word template from F&T
    2. Sections 1-3 written and proofed
    3. Number pages starting from 1
    4. Section headings match template exactly
    5. All tables present (Table 1, 3.1a, 3.1b, 3.1c, 3.1d, 3.1e)
    6. Save as "Part_B1_[Acronym].pdf"
    7. Verify page count ≤ 34

    Step 4: Part B2 - Supporting Documents (by Nov 20)

    1. Org descriptions (max 2 pages total)
      • Each participating org: name, role, experience
      • Associated partners: name and contribution
  • CVs (4 pages max each):
    • Coordinator
    • 3-4 senior supervisors
    • Supervisor commitment letters (if not in CVs)
    • Consortium agreement
    • Partner letters of commitment (signed)
    • Associated partner letters of intent
    • Pre-agreement letters (if DN-JD only)
    • Other supporting docs (Gantt chart, org charts, etc.)

      • Step 5: Final Submission (Nov 24, by 17:00 CET)

      1. Log into F&T Portal as coordinator
      2. Complete Part A online form (all mandatory fields red)
      3. Upload Part B1 PDF
      4. Upload all Part B2 documents in single PDF
      5. Run portal validation: "Check Validation"
      6. Resolve any errors (contact support if needed)
      7. Select "Ready to Submit"
      8. Click "Submit"
      9. Note submission time (must be BEFORE 17:00 CET)
      10. Download and save confirmation receipt
      11. Archive complete submission package locally
